"Burnt Toast"

Aiden Sage

A slice once golden, now charred black,
Dreams of butter can’t bring it back.
Edges curled, a brittle frame,
A quiet witness to the flame.

Crumbs fall like ashes, soft and light,
From a battle lost to heat’s fierce fight.
The scent of smoke, a sharp refrain,
A morning’s hope now tinged with pain.

The toaster sang, the clock struck loud,
A year’s last meal, beneath a shroud.
Distracted thoughts, reflections deep,
Turned bread to coal while hearts did leap.

For New Year’s Eve, with cheers and wine,
We toast the past, the flawed design.
Each burnt mistake, each effort failed,
The storms endured, the ships derailed.

Yet even toast that bears the scars,
Can teach us truths beneath the char.
A lesson whispered through the haze:
That missteps forge the brighter days.

So as we watch the ball descend,
And bid farewell to what must end,
We lift our toast—both burned and bright—
To every stumble, every fight.

For New Year’s comes, a second chance,
A brand-new song, a hopeful dance.
And though the bread may burn once more,
We’ll keep on striving, as before.
